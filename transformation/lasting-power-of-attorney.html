Digital strategy statement

End-to-end digital transformation of the Office for the Public Guardian: this includes applications for Lasting Powers of Attorney by April 2013 and deputyships during 2013-14, and the processes that support them.

From the Ministry of Justice digital strategy – December 2012

Benefits for users

If you need to apply for a lasting power of attorney there is now an online tool to help you. It will explain clearly and plainly what you need to do to validate your application

Business case

Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) allows people to appoint family or friends to take decisions for them if they become unable to do so. There are 2 types of LPA (legal and medical). The present system is paper based and hugely inefficient. The transformation aims to put it online for the first time, cut costs and improve speed and accuracy. Government policy is to increase greatly the number of LPAs (by making them easier and cheaper to obtain) – this means that citizens’ wishes will be known in advance of the type of unfortunate accident that can happen to anyone at any time, and mean there are fewer expensive applications to the Court of Protection which can decide how incapacitated citizens’ affairs are handled.

  • £6m annual admin saving
  • Faster for citizens
  • Citizens wishes known before accidents / emergencies
  • Less fraud
  • Reduced Court of Protection costs
